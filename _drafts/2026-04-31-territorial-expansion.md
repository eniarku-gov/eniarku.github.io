**BALDWIN HOUSE, Glenora** — The Council of State has voted to approve and execute a sovereignty concession contract to acquire approximately 340m<sup>2</sup> of virgin forest territory in Prudentópolis, a municipality located in the southern state of Paraná, Brazil. Under the terms of the two new acts --- the Territories Act (26CRxxxxx) and the Prudentópolis Accession Act (26CRxxxxx) --- the parcel is now considered a dependent territory and will be governed directly from Glenora.

The sovereignty concession contract is a foundational element of the nomadic micronation theory, a micropatriological approach founded on the principle that a micronation may alter its borders without prejudice to the original state. As property or territory belonging to individual citizens comes under or leaves the control of a micronational government, a state's borders can similarly ebb and flow to the extent that micronations can essentialy "wander" across the map. As it pertains to Prudentópolis, the territory was ceded to the Government by a Brazilian local.

Prudentópolis is the center of the Ukrainian community in Brazil and was officially established in August 1906. After road construction began in 1882, approximately 8,000 Ukrainians immigrated to the region through the 1920s, many of whom originated in the Ternopil Oblast (sometimes referred to as Ternopilshchyna or Ternopillia). The Ternopil Oblast is located approximately 100 miles east of the city of Stryi in the Lviv Oblast, the birthplace of Grand Duke Alexander's distant paternal relatives.
